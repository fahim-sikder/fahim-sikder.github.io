Biography

Md Fahim Sikder is a Ph.D. student who works in the field of Data Generation with Privacy under the supervision of Fredrik Heintz at IDA, Linköping University, Sweden. Fahim was a Lecturer in the Computer Science and Engineering department at the Institute of Science, Trade, and Technology (ISTT). He was one of the founding Faculty Member of ISTT ACM Student Chapter (2nd ACM Student Chapter in Bangladesh), Coordinator of HEAP Programming Club, ISTT and, Coach of ACM ICPC team in ISTT.

He is a promising researcher who has several publications also, he has received NVIDIA GPU Grant from NVIDIA to support his research. His research interests include Deep Learning, Computer Vision, Natural Language Processing, Robotics and Data Science.

Md Fahim Sikder conducted several workshops and seminars includes: Workshop on Latex, Week-long training course on Python (Beginning to Advance including Machine Learning). He participated several National and International Contests and earned many titles including “Champion at International Contest on Programming and System Development (ICPSD), 2014”, “Champion at NASA SPACE APPS CHALLENGE 2016” in Rajshahi Region, Bangladesh.

Featured Publications

Md. Fahim Sikder
December 2018 In IJCCI
Bangla Handwritten Digit Recognition & Generation

Handwritten digit or numeral recognition is one of the classical issues in the area of pattern recognition and has seen tremendous advancement because of the recent wide availability of computing resources. Plentiful works have already done on English, Arabic, Chinese, Japanese handwritten script. Some work on Bangla also have been done but there is space for development. From that angle, in this paper, an architecture has been implemented which achieved the validation accuracy of 99.44% on BHAND dataset and outperforms Alexnet and Inception V3 architecture. Beside digit recognition, digit generation is another field which has recently caught the attention of the researchers though not many works have been done in this field especially on Bangla. In this paper, a Semi-Supervised Generative Adversarial Network or SGAN has been applied to generate Bangla handwritten numerals and it successfully generated Bangla digits.
